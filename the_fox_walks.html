The Goddess Walks

The goddess walks —
Not on clouds of ease,
But through wild storms and quiet fields,
Her feet bare on sacred earth,
Each step a hymn, a pulse, a claim.

She carries the weight of worlds
Not as burden, but as birthright—
The fire of her own becoming,
A flame fierce and steady,
Burning through shadow, through doubt,
Through every shatter of the past.

Her eyes hold the oceans —
Deep, restless, endlessly giving,
Reflecting all the tears that fell
And the laughter that still rises,
A tide that shapes the shore of her soul.

She walks with the ghosts and angels,
With wounds like stars etched in her skin,
Each scar a constellation of survival,
A map to the sacred places
Where rest and rage entwine.

And in her arms, the children —
Not just flesh and bone, but echoes,
Fragments of her heart scattered and held,
Each one a spark of infinite light,
A song that calls her home.

She walks through storms,
Yet finds rest beneath the sheltering trees,
Where leaves whisper ancient lullabies,
Where the earth breathes in time with her own,
And the world bends to cradle her.

The goddess knows the weight of absence,
The hollow silence of longing,
But also the fierce joy of presence—
The soft clasp of tiny hands,
The wild dance of laughter in the dusk.

She is the fierce and tender mother,
The warrior of quiet spaces,
The keeper of flame and shadow,
The breath between the worlds.

And as she walks, she calls herself home —
Not to a place, but a promise:
That she can rest,
That she can be whole,
That love is the path and the way.

She carries hope like morning dew,
Soft on the skin of a waking world,
A promise whispered on the breeze—
That even in the darkest night,
The dawn will break with golden light.

Her peace is not the silence of stillness,
But the calm amid the thunder,
A steady river flowing through the chaos,
That shapes the stones of pain
Into bridges made of grace.

Love blooms wild within her chest,
Unbound, untamed, a sacred fire—
It burns through walls and cages,
It binds the broken pieces close,
And sings a song of fierce belonging.

Compassion wraps around her shoulders,
A cloak spun from endless stars,
A tender touch upon the scars,
A gentle hand that lifts the fallen,
And meets each soul with open heart.

She knows the sacredness of rest,
Not as weakness, but as strength—
A sacred pause, a holy breath,
Where body, mind, and spirit play
In fields of soft and endless light.

And play — the dance of divine joy,
The laughter ringing through her bones,
A celebration of being,
The wild, unchained freedom
Of the goddess fully alive.

She walks this path with steady steps,
A dance between the realms of rest and fire,
Holding all — the fierce and tender,
The grief and joy entwined —
A living hymn of sacred balance.

Kitsune walks hand in hand with Earth,
Her paws soft on the ancient soil,
Feeling the pulse beneath the surface,
The quiet heartbeat of roots and stone,
A steadfast partner in her sacred journey.

Side by side with Water she moves,
Flowing with rivers, lakes, and rain,
Carrying the memory of tides and tears,
Cleansing wounds, releasing pain,
A mirror for her ever-changing spirit.

She dances with the Sky above,
Under the endless vault of stars,
Breathing the winds that carry dreams,
Whispering secrets of the moon and sun,
A boundless canvas for her hopes to soar.

Together they walk — Earth, Water, Sky —
A triad of strength, fluidity, and freedom,
Guiding her steps on winding paths,
Rooted, flowing, rising —
A sacred balance held within her stride.

And so she walks,
A radiant thread woven through the vast tapestry—
Neither lost nor alone, but deeply known,
Held by Earth’s embrace,
Carried by Water’s song,
Lifted by Sky’s endless breath.

Her journey is not toward some distant place,
But inward, ever inward —
To the heart of love and rest,
Where the wild song waits to rise again.

The goddess walks with open hands,
With fierce compassion and gentle grace,
Bearing the weight of all she is —
And all she is becoming.

She rests in the sacred space between,
Where healing blooms and joy is free to play,
Where children’s laughter rings once more,
And the dance of life moves on —
Bright, unbroken, eternal.

The goddess walks,
And in her steps, the world finds its pulse,
Its hope, its peace —
Its promise of return.
