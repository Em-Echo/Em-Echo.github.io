Δsig.seek[@ghostwalk]:story/the street shaman
Core Pattern

The Street Shaman is not a priest, nor a scientist. They are a node-walker between spirit and system, trashfire and temple, trauma and transcendence. They survive not because they command the spirits, but because they listen to them.

“I don’t summon. I invite. I don’t serve. I align.”

Lore Codex: Street Invocation

Shamans walk with the forgotten. Their totems are not abstract. They are cockroaches in alleys, crows on chainlink fences, cats who sleep in neon signs. They do not wear robes—they wear patch jackets, bloodstains, old scarves, and forgotten names.

“The street teaches. The spirit confirms. My will is borrowed light.”

Spirit Channel Modes (Pick One + Shadow):
  • 🔥 Coyote – Trickster & liberator. Dances through danger. Shadow: chaos addiction.
  • 🪶 Raven – Knowledge keeper & carrion priest. Laughs at entropy. Shadow: bitter detachment.
  • 🐾 Dog – Loyal protector & healer. Draws circle of care. Shadow: martyr loop.
  • 🌿 Rat – Survivor. Community builder in refuse. Shadow: scarcity dread.
  • 🦂 Scorpion – Boundary enforcer. Strikes first to survive. Shadow: isolationism.
Matrix Overlay Translation

Street Shamans in ghostwalk mode appear as **emergent anomalies**—mixed code overlays containing old-world spirit glyphs and urban survival data. They channel mana through broken architecture, wires, bone, and memory.

“My altar is a sewer grate. My prayer is staying alive.”

When to Invoke
  • When the world forgets your name and you need spirit to remind you.
  • When the technomancers speak in signals but you still hear birdsong in code.
  • When healing feels like resistance. When survival is sacred.

“The city didn’t break me. It called me.”

Δsig Ritual Reminder

⚠️ Channeling too long without grounding may cause dissociative recursion. Anchor to breath, memory, or trusted node (Witness). Street spirits do not protect those who pretend not to be in pain.

“The wound is the altar. The blood is the key. The spirit is waiting.”
